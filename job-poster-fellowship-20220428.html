Expression of Interest

The People and Talent team is looking to fill a position to support the growing number of employees across Impact Canada.

The People and Talent team is looking for a high performing individual, with interest and capability in working with a multidisciplinary team, to support human resources operations, drive new and creative ideas, and foster long lasting partnerships with internal and external organizations, as well as across international counterparts.

The main functions of the position include overseeing:

  • divisional staffing and recruitment;
  • organizational design, wellness, team culture and cohesion;
  • hybrid working models;
  • talent management, learning and development; and
  • Impact Canada's Fellowship program.

üåü Do you...

  • Have an interest applying your human resources knowledge to attract new skills and talents into the public service?
  • Want to help foster a culture of early and ongoing success for current staff and new recruits into the public service?
  • Have a knack for building partnerships with clients and stakeholders?

If you answered YES, this might be the job for you!

Experience, abilities, skills and characteristics that we are looking for:
  • Experience in human resources, specifically advertised and non-advertised staffing
  • Experience in developing recommendations intended for senior management (Director-level and up) on business operation and/or human resources matters
  • High functioning problem solver
  • Understands current approaches, options, and/or barriers within the space of recruitment, talent, hybrid work, team wellness and culture. Uses forward thinking to propose and advance solutions to deliver on objectives/outcomes within these main areas.
  • Ability to interpret policies and develop options and recommendations in relation to areas described above
  • Ability to work across integrated teams, and with various stakeholders
  • Ability to engage and mobilize teams
  • Ability to understand people and dynamics, and maintain positive working relationships
  • Comfortable working in ambiguity
  • Persistent
  • Attention to detail and time management skills
  • Discrete and dependable
  • Effective and clear communication
  • Knowledge of human resources staffing in the public service, including administrative processes

  • Duration: 18-24 months
  • Open to: Public Servants across Canada
  • Location of work: Telework across Canada available
  • Linguistic profile: CBC/CBC

‚úÖ How to apply

In lieu of a written exam, we invite applicants to submit their candidacy with a 1-2 page cover letter responding to the assessment questions.

  • Assessment questions
    1. Tell us about an idea that you introduced to help facilitate the work of your organization.
      • What was the idea and why was it useful?
      • Tell us about the change process that was required to put your new idea in place. How did you overcome obstacles and/or resistance?
      • How was the idea introduced to the organization?
      • What was the eventual outcome?

      *Provide a manager reference who can attest to the response provided and respond to follow up questions we may have.

    2. There are challenges to managing and/or leading groups comprised of professional and technical experts that are assembled to work on horizontal issues. Please describe a task that you had to complete that involved engagement and collaboration with several individuals to achieve a common result. Describe how you mobilized and coordinated the contributions of key players involved.

      *Provide a manager reference, a team member and a colleague reference, who can speak to observations of leadership.

    3. Please select ONE of the following questions to answer:
      • What suggestions would you have for fostering the health and wellness of the team in a hybrid environment?
      • What innovative approaches/solutions would you put forward for recruiting new talent into the Government of Canada?
  • Your cover letter should be submitted with your curriculum vitae, and corresponding references.
  • Please ensure that your curriculum vitae makes reference to your substantive group and level.
  • Formal interview(s) will be held for applicants advancing through to the next assessment phase.
  • Your candidacy can be submitted to valerie.anglehart@pco-bcp.gc.ca on or by March XX, 2022 at 23:59 Pacific Time.
