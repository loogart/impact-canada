(Wo)Mandate

The Impact and Innovation Unit (IIU) takes a “mission-driven” approach in advancing initiatives that achieve outcomes, value and impact for citizens. Through the strategic use of outcomes-based methods, working horizontally across government and sectors, and relentlessly focusing on implementation, we can solve some of the biggest challenges facing our society.

Under the Impact Canada brand, we work with partners within and outside of government to mainstream the use of challenges, behavioural science, and pay-for-success funding models. The promise of these methods is that they focus effort not on the process or outputs of an initiative, but rather on clearly defining the sought after outcome and ensuring there is a multisectoral approach to achieving it. In this context, we strongly believe that robust engagement, co-design, and co-development is the best path to get us there.

As part of Impact Canada’s work in the development and implementation of challenges, the department is using a quasi-experimental methodology to assess the impact of challenges, providing information into what types of organizations participate in challenges, as well as insights into whether challenges help to stimulate and help grow innovative market entrants (e.g. new firm-types, new products).

Key Activities

The IIU is looking for a student to assist our team with the following tasks:

  • Support the implementation of an impact measurement framework to study the effectiveness of challenges prizes as an innovation financing mechanism
  • Support with identifying indicators to be used for impact measurement of challenge prizes
  • Review and update quantitative and qualitative data collection tools
  • Conduct quantitative data analysis (using software such as Excel, SPSS, STATA or others)
  • Support in collecting and analyzing qualitative data, including writing of findings
  • Write methodology reports, analysis reports, case studies, and policy notes/briefs
  • Develop presentations using PowerPoint or other presentation software
  • Coordinate with other Government of Canada departments
  • Undertake other tasks as required
