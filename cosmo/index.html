COSMO Canada

Interim Report | Published 2020-10-19

Key findings from the implementation of the WHO Behavioural Insights tool on COVID-19 in Canada.

+ Table of contents

Background

With support from PCO’s Public Opinion Research (POR) Unit and endorsement from communications and policy partners across the Government of Canada – in particular within the health portfolio – Impact Canada launched the COVID-19 Snapshot Monitoring (COSMO) study in Canada in early April 2020.

+ Learn more

Study objectives

Overall, research questions relate to:

Risk perceptions

How changes in risk perceptions may relate to knowledge and misinformation;

Participant Awareness

Whether participants report that they are aware of specific response measures and whether being aware of them influences risk perceptions;

Preparedness and behaviours

Whether risk perceptions are related to preparedness and the use of protective behaviours;

Knowledge vs. misinformation

Knowledge and misinformation about preparedness and preventive measures and whether the level of knowledge is related to certain sources of information; and

Government help reactions

Reactions to announcements and programs from the Government of Canada to help combat the COVID-19 outbreak, and its associated effects.;

Methodology

Areas of focus

An academic advisory committee - with expertise in infectious disease, behavioural science, public health and epidemiology - provides ongoing feedback that informs Wave- to-Wave survey adaptations.

Wave 1

April 11-15

Focused on establishing a strong baseline understanding of Canadians’ current knowledge base (e.g., symptoms, virus spread, virus recovery), perceptions and attitudes.

+ Learn more

Wave 2

April 11-15

Questions were added that focused on the ongoing mental health and wellbeing challenges Canadians may be experiencing, as well as changes to their existing behavioural routines during the pandemic (e.g., shopping behaviours).

+ Learn more

Wave 3

May 5-10

Questions were added that focused on the ongoing mental health and wellbeing challenges Canadians may be experiencing, as well as changes to their existing behavioural routines during the pandemic (e.g., shopping behaviours).

Wave 4

May 26 - June 1

Questions were added that focused on how behaviours have shifted in balancing work and family priorities throughout this time of transition.

Wave 5

June 23-28

A continued focus on vaccine confidence, returning to “normal” behaviours, travel intentions, pandemic fatigue, along with the reopening of the economy and its associated impacts.

Increased the focus on vaccine confidence, consumer behaviour, along with a continued focus on returning to “normal” behaviours, travel intentions and pandemic fatigue.
