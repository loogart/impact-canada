October 28-November 1, 2020

Vaccine Study 2: The Effects of Messaging and Messengers on Vaccine Perceptions

This study used an online survey to assess the impact of various messaging approaches and messengers on Canadians' intentions to be vaccinated against COVID-19.

Summary

The Impact and Innovation Unit's second deep dive study on vaccines. A broadly representative sample of approximately 1,600 Canadians completed an online survey to assess the impact of various messaging approaches and messengers on intentions to be vaccinated against COVID-19.

Three messaging approaches focused on communicating information about vaccine safety were delivered through three messengers. The study assessed intention to vaccinate against COVID-19, confidence in Canada's vaccine development process, comprehension of vaccine messaging, and fears and discomfort surrounding a COVID-19 vaccine. 

Key insights

Key insights revealed through this study suggest that both messaging type and messenger impact vaccination intentions, with a personal narrative delivered by a close friend being the most effective at increasing vaccination intentions. The study also revealed that giving Canadians a better view into how a COVID-19 vaccine is developed is helpful in ensuring widespread understanding of vaccine-related messaging.
